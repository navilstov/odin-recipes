Description

Fish amok (known as amok trei in Khmer) is the national dish of Cambodia. It's best described as a steamed fish curry with a uniquely light, mousse-like texture

Key Ingredients:

    • Fish
      lemon grass
      galangal
      turmeric
      kaffir lime
      garlic
      shallots
      coconuts milk
      eggs
      Banana leaf

    • Cooking Step

    • Make the Kroeung paste – Blend lemongrass, galangal, turmeric, kaffir lime leaves, garlic, shallots, and dried chilies into a fine paste.
      Prepare the custard – Mix the kroeung paste with coconut milk and beaten eggs until smooth.
      Add the fish – Gently fold in bite-sized pieces of firm white fish (snakehead, cod, or snapper).
      Prepare banana leaf bowls – Shape banana leaves into small cups and secure with toothpicks.
      Fill and steam – Pour the fish mixture into the banana leaf bowls. Steam over medium heat for 15–20 minutes until the custard is set and firm.
      Garnish and serve – Drizzle coconut cream on top, garnish with sliced chili and lime leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice.
